My Dream 2019 Oscar Ballot

Not trying to say that I'm always right, but I am

By Carson Zhang

A predictable night at the Oscars pleasantly surprised when Parasite unprecedently won Best Director and Best Picture over 1917. Considering my contemptable opinion of most of the Academy's choices, it was easily my favorite of their choices in recent years. However, for every Bong Joon-ho victory there was a Joker or Ford v Ferrari win, and The Irishman wound up the only Best Picture-nominated film that didn't win anything. The Oscars are supposed to honor great movies from around the world that actually push the medium forward, yet most of their choices encourage studios to make the same tired fare year after year. Annually, a couple of "brutally honest" voters explain their choices to the press, which is a worthwhile reminder that the vast majority of the voting body is actually just really stupid. In the spirit of creativity and innovation in the wake of a celebration of mediocrity and consistency, I feel obligated to share my own provocative choices for the Oscar line-up. If I was an Academy voter and not a teenage kid who's sorely missing some teeth, these are the films of 2019 that I would nominate, ranked preferentially in descending order.

*Indicates a correlation with the Academy
